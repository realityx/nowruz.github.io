Happy Nowruz!



Spring is here again and Nowruz marking the new year and new beginning. Nowruz is a ritual dating back to at least the 6th century BCE. Nowruz is observed on 20 March this year and celebrated by many countries across the globe including Afghanistan, Azerbaijan, India, Iran, Iraq, Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, Pakistan, Tajikistan, Turkey, Turkmenistan, and Uzbekistan.



At the exact time of vernal equinox, people gather around Haft-Sin (set-up table with seven symbolic items as symbols of rebirth, health, love, prosperity, patience, and life) and celebrate the glorification of growth and vigor in Mother Nature. On the day of Nowruz, there is feasting, visiting family and friends and exchanging gifts.



HAFTSIN
Arrangement of seven symbolic items whose names start with the letter "س"
Below you can see several items of Haftsin and what each item mean.



Goldfish: the symbol of progress



Hyacinth: the symbol of spring's arrival



Apple: the symbol of beauty





Candle: the symbol of enlightenment



Coin: the symbol of wealth and prosperity



Sumac: the symbol of sunrise.



Sprouting /Grass: the symbol of rebirth and growth



Garlic: the symbol of health and medicine



Eggs: the symbol of fertility
